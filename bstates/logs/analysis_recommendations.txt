Scheme_E basis-state directory. It is saved here for provenance: it records

WHAT WAS PRODUCED
----------------------------------------------------------------------------
The 04_ pipeline reads the per-bstate cpptraj pcoords, assigns basins from the
pcoords alone (k-means(2) + a continuous folded<->unfolded margin; transition =
partly-formed stem held at intermediate G1-C14 MinDist), selects diverse seed
subsets by farthest-point sampling (FPS), and emits:

----------------------------------------------------------------------------
ARE THE RECOMMENDED SCHEMES "OPTIMAL"? Mostly yes, with honest caveats.
----------------------------------------------------------------------------
Two layers, judged separately:

The selection algorithm (within a basin): near-optimal, not provably optimal.
Maximizing the minimum pairwise distance of a k-subset is the k-center /
max-min diversity problem -- NP-hard. Greedy FPS is the standard 2-approximation
(Gonzalez 1985): worst case never more than 2x from optimal, and on smooth
low-dimensional clouds like this it is effectively optimal. The criterion
itself -- max-min spread in standardized pcoord space -- is the correct lever
per S4.11.6 (within-basin spread excites psi_3/psi_4), so the objective is well
chosen even if the solver is a heuristic.

The scheme designs (which basins, what ratio): theoretically sound, but with
free parameters that are conventions, not optima.
  - N = 8 per basin is a round number, not an optimized count. The saturation
    curves show 8 is already past diminishing returns on the folded side.
  - Basin assignment uses k-means(2), which forces exactly two clusters and is
    sensitive to feature scaling; it agrees with the labels well, so it is
    defensible, but it is not a unique partition.
  - The transition thresholds (1<=nBP<=4, 3<MinDist<25 A) are read off the
    histograms by eye. Reasonable, but movable.
None of this is wrong -- it is the difference between "optimal" and
"well-justified by the theory with sensible defaults."

----------------------------------------------------------------------------
DO YOU NEED TO PULL MORE OR DIFFERENT STRUCTURES? Yes -- and which.
----------------------------------------------------------------------------
The three basins are wildly unequal in diversity:

  basin           n     pool spread   marginal gain by seed 8
  folded_side     32    0.415         0.29 (flat)
  transition      21    1.103         ~0.66
  unfolded_side   17    2.725         0.92 (still climbing)

- The folded basin is OVER-sampled but REDUNDANT. 32 structures, lowest spread
  by far, and the FPS subset spread FALLS as you add seeds (0.805 at k=4 ->
  0.613 at k=12) -- the signature of adding near-duplicates. fig_div_redundancy
  shows it as a uniform dark block. More folded structures from the same
  sampling will NOT help. What you need are structurally DIFFERENT folded
  substates -- varied loop / sugar-pucker conformations, alternative stem
  registers, the G9-syn vs anti distinction -- not more near-native snapshots.
  A higher-temperature or differently-restrained sampling of the folded well
  would do more than 30 more frames from the current one.

- The unfolded basin is the only well-covered one, and its marginal gain is
  still high at seed 8 -- you could justify MORE unfolded seeds here.

- The barrier is genuinely under-sampled (see next).

So the answer is not "pull more" uniformly; it is REBALANCE: fewer (but more
diverse) folded, keep/expand unfolded, and actively go get barrier structures
you do not currently have.

THE COMMITTOR GAP (the thing to flag hardest):
fig_committor_gap shows fold_frac is sharply bimodal with an essentially EMPTY
window from ~0.4 to ~0.7. Of the 21 "transition" structures, all but one sit at
fold_frac 0.75-0.93 -- these are folded-committed states with frayed/opening
termini, not true transition-state-ensemble members. Only struct 53
(fold_frac 0.37) is on the unfolded-committed side, and there is NOTHING at the
committor ~0.5 barrier top. Your "transition band" is really "folded basin
edge." To resolve the actual barrier (sharpen psi_2's node and the FES saddle,
S4.10.1) you must GENERATE near-committor-0.5 structures -- e.g. string /
steered-MD or committor-selected frames from a pilot WE run -- because they are
not in the current 70.

----------------------------------------------------------------------------
SHOULD YOU RUN SCHEME B?
----------------------------------------------------------------------------
Yes -- B over A as your first/primary run, with one expectation set. B = A's
both-basins coverage (the binding constraint, S4.10) PLUS transition seeds, and
adding the transition seeds is essentially free: it does not touch psi_2
recovery (they sit near the node) while improving pi-hat/p-hat coverage and FES
resolution along the pathway. There is no scenario where A beats B given you
already have these structures prepped. Caveat from the committor gap: B's
transition seeds are folded-edge structures, so B sharpens the folded-side
APPROACH to the barrier well but will not illuminate the true saddle until you
supply genuine barrier-top structures. Run B now; treat barrier-top sampling as
the next acquisition target, not a reason to delay B.

Do NOT add a unidirectional FOLDED run (cancels psi_2, S4.14). C
(unidirectional unfolded) is worth running later and pooling with B, checking
eta_2 > 1/sqrt(N_runs) before trusting the pooled estimate. D stays a separate,
down-weighted higher-mode probe -- never equal-weight pooled into the psi_2
corpus.

----------------------------------------------------------------------------
SCHEME E -- balanced, barrier-spanning, 13 seeds (THIS DIRECTORY)
----------------------------------------------------------------------------
Design: 4 FPS-diverse folded + 4 FPS-diverse unfolded + 5 transition selected
for proximity to committor 0.5 AND a genuinely opening terminus (MinDist > 6 A),
then FPS within that barrier-core for spread. That last filter matters --
without it the picker grabbed struct 54 (MinDist 3.6 A, basically folded); with
it the transition seeds span MinDist 7.4-22 A and fold_frac 0.37-0.82.

  role            structs (orig)     fold_frac    notes
  folded (4)      4, 11, 35, 50      0.89-0.92    FPS, spread 0.805 -- HIGHER
                                                  than A's 8-seed 0.679, because
                                                  dropping to 4 avoids the
                                                  redundant tail
  unfolded (4)    64, 52, 60, 65     0.13-0.19    FPS, spread 3.11
  transition (5)  42, 53, 37, 22, 31 0.37-0.82    barrier-core, MinDist 7.4-22 A,
                                                  spread 1.49

E deliberately trades per-basin seed COUNT for balance and barrier coverage --
using only 4 folded seeds is right because the folded saturation curve is flat
past ~4. Honest limitation: even E's transition seeds top out around the
folded-committed edge plus the lone struct 53, because the true committor-0.5
barrier top is NOT in your structure set. E is the best barrier-spanning design
AVAILABLE from the current 70; closing the committor gap requires generating new
structures, not re-selecting existing ones.

In this directory the 13 seeds are renamed sequentially: struct_0..struct_12.
struct_mapping.log records each new index <- original struct_X <- bstate_id.
